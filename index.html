Game Overview

Tennis is a racket sport that can be played individually against a single opponent (singles) or between two teams of two players each (doubles). Each player uses a tennis racket that is strung with cord to strike a hollow rubber ball covered with felt over or around a net and into the opponent's court. The object of the game is to maneuver the ball in such a way that the opponent is not able to play a valid return. The player who is unable to return the ball will not gain a point, while the opposite player will.

Highlights

Big 4 Players

  • Roger Federer: Known as "The Maestro," Federer holds the record for the most Wimbledon titles in history.
  • Rafael Nadal: Dubbed the "King of Clay," Nadal has an unprecedented 14 French Open titles.
  • Novak Djokovic: The "Djoker" has achieved a Career Grand Slam twice, a feat few have accomplished.
  • Andy Murray: Murray ended Great Britain's 77-year wait for a Wimbledon men's singles champion in 2013.

Grand Slam Tournaments

  • Wimbledon: The only Grand Slam played on grass, it's known for its strict dress code requiring players to wear all white.
  • US Open: Held in New York, it's the final Grand Slam of the year and features the largest tennis stadium in the world, Arthur Ashe Stadium.
  • French Open (Roland-Garros): Famous for its red clay courts, it's considered the most physically demanding Grand Slam due to its slower surface.
  • Australian Open: Known as the "Happy Slam," it is the first Grand Slam of the year and features innovative extreme heat policies for player safety.

Types of Tennis Shots

  • Forehand: A shot made by swinging the racket across one's body with the hand moving palm-first.
  • Backhand: A shot made by swinging the racket away from one's body with the back of the hand leading.
  • Serve: The shot that starts a point, usually hit overhead from behind the baseline.
  • Volley: A shot hit before the ball bounces on the ground, typically played near the net.
  • Tweener: A trick shot played between the legs, usually when chasing down a lob.
